What marketing campaigns (like "Scan to win") have the best engagement, and where should QR codes be placed?

The best marketing campaigns, like "Scan to win," have the highest engagement when QR codes are large enough for easy scanning and placed in accessible, high-visibility locations such as at eye level on printed materials, TV screens, or other media where users are most likely to notice and scan them; Flowcodes with a call to action foster 2X the engagement compared to standard codes.

Is it necessary to use unique QR codes for different placements and can their traffic be identified?

It is best practice to use a unique QR code on each placement in order to track the performance of unique interactions through UTM parameters and our detailed scan analytics, ensuring you always know how and where your traffic is flowing. A singular QR can be used across multiple placements, but analytics by placement will not be available.

What are reusable or dynamic QR codes for and how is their traffic identified?

Reusable or dynamic QR codes are used to track and manage customer interactions by linking to various destinations and dynamically updating the content behind the QR codes; their traffic is identified through unique tracking technology, such as FlowID, which captures detailed user activities and interactions across multiple touchpoints using email or anonymous identifiers, first-party cookies, and integrations with analytics systems​​​​​​.
